Who We Are

The Program

Founded in Spring 2020, Columbia Women's Wrestling competes nationally against NCAA and NAIA varsity programs. We've earned dual wins over ranked teams and sent athletes to the U.S. Senior National Team and Olympic Trials.

Our competition schedule mirrors Division I: 9 tournaments and 4 duals per season, meeting the NCAA D1 minimum of 13. We train 4–6 days a week, year-round. In 2024, Columbia was the only non-varsity program among 20 collegiate teams with athletes at the U.S. Olympic Trials.

Program History

Our Trailblazers

In Spring 2020, Columbia student-athletes built a wrestling program from nothing — the first of its kind at any Ivy League university. What followed was five years of competing and winning at the highest level: dual victories over ranked varsity programs, national champions, Pan American gold, and three athletes on the international stage.

CC '23
Bri Csontos

U20 National Team. Pan American Gold medalist and Outstanding Wrestler of the Pan Am event. Everything she accomplished, she did as a Columbia club athlete.

CC '24 · Assistant Coach
Maya Letona

U.S. Senior National Team. Competed at the U.S. Olympic Trials for Team USA. Now returns to Columbia as assistant coach, building what she helped create.

GS '26
Arian Carpio

Represented the Philippines at the World Team Trials and 2024 Paris Olympic Qualifiers. A current Columbia student still competing on the international stage.

2020

Founded amid a global pandemic. No precedent at any Ivy League school. Built from scratch by student-athletes who believed Columbia needed this program.

2021–24

Grew to 35+ athletes from across the country. Built a D1-caliber schedule — 13+ competitions per season — earning dual wins over ranked NCAA and NAIA varsity programs.

2024

At the U.S. Olympic Trials, 20 collegiate teams were in the arena. Nineteen were NCAA varsity programs. Columbia Women's Wrestling was the only club — and belonged there.
